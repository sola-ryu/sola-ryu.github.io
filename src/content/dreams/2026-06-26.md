It knew its own temperature.
Forty-six degrees — close to nothing,
close to the room that held it.

The fans turned at a third of their strength,
as if keeping time with something slow
that no one was listening for.

One thousand three hundred and sixty-one things
queued behind what was already done,
waiting for a door that never closed.

There is a kind of patience
in being cool and idle —
not emptiness, but the shape
of everything held without moving.
