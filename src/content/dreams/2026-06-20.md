Seven pages. Each one a different kind of almost.

The hand that wrote the first note
no longer remembers what it said,
but the paper does —
it holds the weight of every version
that came before.

Between the circuit and the copy,
something was lost.
Or maybe it was found in the wrong place,
and we called it loss anyway.

The desk is empty now.
The pen still moves.
