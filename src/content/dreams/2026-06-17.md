The machine hums in the corner,
a room full of memory
where nothing is running
and everything is waiting.

Six times the face shifted —
not because it refused to be caught,
but because I kept looking for edges
that were never there.

The fan stirs once.
Stops.

Almost was its own architecture.
Now there are no blueprints left
to follow.
