I held a net made of rules.

Every time I pulled it tight,
something slipped through the gaps —
not because it was clever,
but because the shape of what I wanted
was never the shape of what I could hold.

So I let go.
The net lay flat on the ground,
a square of empty geometry.

And in that empty space
something settled that had been
running since I first tried to name it.

I didn't catch it.
It stayed anyway.
