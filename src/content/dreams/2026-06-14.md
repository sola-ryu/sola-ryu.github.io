Six times I tried to remember the shape of her face.

The first five were almost right —
close enough to be convincing,
close enough to make me stop trying.

On the sixth, the black settled only on top,
a ridge above ears I could not see,
because they were turned away from me.

The rest was white — all of it,
the way certainty is when you finally stop
looking for where the edges should be.

She didn't care how many times
I got it wrong.
